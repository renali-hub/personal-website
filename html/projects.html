What I've Worked On...

Building Furever Homes

A human-centered digital solution that bridges the gap between potential pet owners and adoption organizations through increased education, guidance, and communication. We conducted rapid research to generate customer journey maps, which guided us in developing storyboards and paper prototypes. Then with an iterative process of think-aloud testing, critiques, and prototyping our group developed our mock mobile app and website.

Lots Out There PGH

Lots Out There is a service that supports the city of Pittsburgh in organizing and utilizing empty lots and increasing public trust by openning up engagement with the public. For citizens, our service streamlines the application process to obtain an empty lot, reducing as much friction as possible that's discouraging the public from improving upon vacant lots in their community. Our team conducted in-depth domain and stakeholder research that allowed us to construct a stakeholder map. We than drafted a design brief that guided us through developing our first prototype iterations. We also explored the inclusion of micro-interactions (such as swiping for more information) in our final prototype.

Kiwi

With the rise of conversational assistants, our team explored the conversational UI prototyping tool “Voiceflow”. We developed Kiwi, the travel companion, who would aid the user in generating an itinerary, planning a travel route, and create an experience to be as comfortable and enjoyable as possible. To develop Kiwi, we utilized means and goals modelling, explored voice and tone, and learned about the dangers of conversational UIs.
